## Project Overview
A US-based company with thousands of employees across multiple departments needs to better understand its workforce dynamics, including employee profile, performance, satisfaction, and workforce movement.

As a **Data Analyst**, I was tasked with analyzing HR data and building an interactive dashboard to monitor key workforce metrics, identify turnover patterns, and support HR team.

## Objectives
- Understand current workforce condition  
- Identify turnover patterns and key drivers  
- Analyze workforce movement (hiring & turnover trends)

## Key Insights
- The company shows strong workforce growth, with turnover stabilizing after peaking in 2015, likely due to improved retention strategies.  
- The main turnover reasons are **career opportunities elsewhere**, **working hours**, and **job dissatisfaction**, consistently appearing across periods.  
- Software Engineering has the highest workload and turnover, indicating potential burnout risk, IT/IS also shows similar pressure.  
- The current workforce is dominated by productive-age employees, mostly female, with generally high performance, engagement, and satisfaction.  
- Turnover is most common among **female employees aged 45-49 and single**, highlighting a specific at-risk group.  

## Recommendations
- Improve retention strategies by addressing key turnover drivers such as career growth, workload balance, and job satisfaction.  
- Implement workload redistribution or hiring plans in high-pressure departments (Software Engineering & IT/IS).  
- Develop targeted retention programs for mid-to-senior employees (age 45-49), especially focusing on career development and well-being.  
- Maintain and strengthen existing engagement and satisfaction programs to sustain overall workforce performance.  
- Enhance monitoring using dashboard insights to proactively detect turnover risks.  

## Output
